Wheelchair Extension System

Mechanical Design & Prototyping

Project Overview

This engineering project focused on developing a structural modification system designed to enhance the functionality, stability, and versatility of standard wheelchairs. By implementing custom structural alterations, the system improves mechanical reliability and overall user accessibility.

Key Engineering Objectives

  • CAD Design & Development: Utilizing 3D modeling tools (such as Autodesk Inventor) to draft the structural ribs, components, and interlocking mechanical features of the extension assembly.
  • Structural Integrity Analysis: Optimizing component configurations, material thickness, and rib spacing to ensure the system can withstand realistic mechanical loading conditions.
  • Prototyping & Verification: Evaluating spatial tolerances, cross-sectional stress resistance, and assembly mechanics using physical prototypes or detailed digital simulations.

Reflections & Outcomes

The final iteration successfully demonstrated how targeted mechanical enhancements can solve real-world mobility challenges. Developing this extension system highlighted the critical balance between physical structural reinforcement, strict weight restrictions, and precise assembly manufacturing parameters.
